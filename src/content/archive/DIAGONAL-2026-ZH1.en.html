Location: ZG / Zigong

"Trace-Hunting" is a visual-intervention activity (tracking, tracing, copying) initiated by Ni Weihua, in which everyone can participate and which targets all kinds of trace textures; its result is to strengthen or superimpose the visual resolution of traces. "Trace-Hunting" takes easel painting as its initial methodological experiment. Since May 2018, using old buildings, ruin walls, newly built roughcast houses, rocks, and old wooden boards in social public spaces as carriers, and using natural (weathered, corroded) and human-written superimposed traces as image resources, it foregrounds those forgotten and overlooked trace images in social public space through extreme contrasting-colour backfilling, hard-edge tracing of textures to amplify contrast, and other "operations" that highlight traces — turning them into a new image and a new visual landscape that has been appropriated and intervened upon.

From June 2018 to December 2025, 365 events have been held in 34 locations worldwide, with 2,400 participants.

The composition of traces is diverse and ever-changing. It involves both space and time, and many codes are hidden within.

Natural Codes | 自然密码

This is the entropy increase of the universe, displaying the original trace codes (such as imprints on coastal reefs) from the formative period of the solar system and the Earth; it is also slowly evolving, and contains the codes of animal and plant evolutionary traces from various periods.

Humanistic & Social Codes | 人文与社会密码

Codes of traces left behind by the course of human evolution and various activities; codes of traces formed by social change and the interaction, cooperation, or conflict and contest among different strata.

Aesthetic Codes | 美学密码

From creating images back to viewing, one perceives the aesthetic codes of traces in the act of viewing — whether human-made traces, cracks, weathered scars, or traces formed by mutual crossing and superimposition, all possess aesthetic qualities between the regular and the wild, between the seamless and textural detail; sometimes they can give rise to aesthetic spectacles beyond human reach.

Artist | 艺术家

Ni Weihua (1962–), Chinese contemporary artist, born in Shanghai, graduated from Shanghai Light Industry College. He is one of the early practitioners who promoted the development of contemporary art in China and Shanghai. His practice spans painting, installation, photography, video, and social intervention. With "art intervening in society" as his core concept, his works often focus on the relationship between landscape and the individual in the process of urbanisation; representative works include the "Landscape Wall", "Keywords", and "Trace-Hunting" series. He has participated in the Venice Biennale, the Marrakech Biennale, the China Contemporary Art Documentary Exhibition, and many other major domestic and international exhibitions, and is hailed as an artist of "the perpetual neo-vanguard".
