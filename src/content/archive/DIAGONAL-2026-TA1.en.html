By Alain de Botton | 阿兰·德波顿（Alain de Botton）著

What is travel?

Some say it is escape, others say it is arrival; some say it is the collection of landscapes, others say it is the exile of the self. Some treat travel as a grand festival, meticulously prepared, then anticipating that single moment of blooming; others treat travel as a kind of everyday life, as natural as breathing, measuring out a diary of their own with their footsteps over long years.

Recently a friend wrote to me: saying that distant places, it turns out, cannot solve reality either.

"It turns out that distant places cannot solve reality after all."

Is the distant place we long for what travel is? Or can it be broken down into very small units, closely bound up with our lives?

The first episode of the "Travel, Life and Art" series, co-curated by Diagonal, LANGKONG. TIMETRAVEL, and Dengxiashuo, takes us to read this small classic book.

About the Book | 关于《旅行的艺术》

This is not a travelogue in the traditional sense. De Botton deliberately writes travel as a spiritual experience, rather than physical exertion.

De Botton infuses travel with extensive reflection on culture, art, and even the meaning of life itself. He writes of the spiritual pleasure of an aeroplane's take-off — "for the swift ascent of the aeroplane is the best symbol of the turning point in life's realisation"; he writes of the quiet brought by clouds — "below us lies the place of our fears and sorrows, where our enemies and colleagues are, and now they are all on the ground, insignificant and of no consequence."

Why Recommend | 为什么推荐这本书

It answers a fundamental question: why do we travel?

From Flaubert's longing for the exoticism of the Orient, to Humboldt's pure power of curiosity, De Botton reminds us: travel requires more initiative and knowledge reserve, rather than wandering about with a travel guidebook in hand and an empty head, queuing at every stop.

It tells us: the ultimate purpose of travel is not escape, but return. "Everyday life" and "going away to travel" are not opposites. The key lies in our state of mind. The highest art of travel is learning how to live vividly and profoundly without having to go far.

It offers an ideal way of living. De Botton invites us to cultivate a curious, keen, and imaginative state of mind, so as to transform every walk and every gaze into a miniature, spiritual journey of the soul.

Diagonal | 对角线计划

The Diagonal originated from bodily movement from northeast to southwest, forming a corresponding line from the "grassland, forest plain" to the "mountain highland" area on the Chinese map, echoing Hu Huanyong's line from Heihe in the northeast to Tengchong in the southwest. From physical geography to human economy, from nearly a hundred years ago to contemporary society, from agricultural tradition to industrial prosperity, we try to find and relate common images, symbols, ideas and spirits, presenting the diverse features of the two regions in the form of art, combining practice and theory, and trying to summarise effective methods to promote and influence the evolution of local ecology.

The project is presented in diverse forms, including but not limited to sharing sessions, art festivals, exhibitions, workshops, field investigations, residencies, and publications. The Diagonal currently has art spaces in Zigong, Sichuan Province (Huojingtuo Creators' Dormitory, SaltArt Space, Carbon Studio, etc.), and maintains long-term cooperative relationships with numerous domestic and international institutions and spaces.
