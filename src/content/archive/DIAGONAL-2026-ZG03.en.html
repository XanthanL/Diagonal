Exhibition Introduction

“The Salt of Life”: Landscape, Polyphony, Aftertaste

Curator: Kerribin / 策展人：孙晓鸣 Kerribin

An exhibition is both a performance during a residency and a means of self-discovery through creative practice.

As the four-month Zigong Huojingtuo artistic residency project draws to a close, and as this historic site in southern Sichuan — imbued with complex flavours of saltiness, spiciness, heat, and moisture — embodies a renewed summer spirit, after six months of dialogue between the visiting artists’ sense of unfamiliarity with the salt capital’s legacy shaped by millennia of labour and their repeated encounters with the familiar textures of local life, we present an artistic feast that reveals our multifaceted emotions and space-time imaginings toward this city.

Locality is a perceptual vocabulary that artists continually grapple with during creation; it also echoes a buzzword that has emerged frequently in recent years — local knowledge. First-time artists arrive with indirect knowledge, entering this mountain city together with their senses. In the process of investigation, these semantic cells synthesise with the direct experience of local guides, forming new place-based cognition and outputting later as “individual place imagery.” Landscape here refers to the unique image of Zigong in each person’s mind — both geographical and humanistic. In the city’s obvious sensory rewriting narrative, the “city in the sky” seems distant and enchanting; its series of gigantic limbs intrude dominantly into the visual field, yet leave behind marginal remnants no longer inhabited by life. Artists attempt to record the stories of this disappearance through sound, experiencing a different kind of storytelling from another world. We hear the breathing of some underground gas, whose anchored image is almost unrelated to the hawking sounds above ground, which are more commonly appropriated by artists as creative material. Besides this, the everyday we hear seems to fill the entire cranial cavity like a polyphony, stirring sensory materials to the extreme, with spiciness as the finishing touch of this cooking. Yet artists have their own kingdoms of flavour; they have already encountered many unexpected people, events, and things, digested these delicately, and ultimately released the myriad artistic aftertastes of this place.

Exhibition Overview

“The Salt of Life”

Initiator: Vivienne Peng / 发起人：彭玮雯 Vivienne Peng

“Salt” seems to be both the beginning and anchor point of Zigong life. Salt is not only a material substance, but also a form of memory, deeply embedded in everyday life. It connects Zigong’s industrial history, entering contemporary life, bodily experience, and public space.

On 27 June 2026, The Salt of Life: Contemporary Art Exhibition on Zigong Salt Culture will open at Ziliujing Old Street. This exhibition presents the phased outcomes of Diagonal’s The Salt of Life 1.0 & 2.0: Zigong Salt Culture Research and Creation Residency Programmes. Centred on contemporary art, it includes installations, photography, film, live art, documentary displays, and site-specific material experiments, featuring approximately 26 works or groups of works. The exhibition brings together the outcomes of fieldwork, research, artistic creation, and public activities carried out in the first half of 2026 around Zigong’s salt culture, industrial heritage, urban transformation, and community memory.

As a phase of Diagonal’s art project around Zigong salt culture, this exhibition gathers more than twenty artists, researchers, and creators from different regions and backgrounds. Taking Zigong’s urban salt culture as an index, we enter salt industry sites, old street spaces, industrial communities, markets, the Fuxi riverbank, and everyday life scenes, conducting investigation, research, creation, and exchange around salt culture development, industrial heritage, urban transformation, and community memory.

The Salt of Life is not only a project about the cultural history of salt, but a practice of re-entering the contemporary local cultural scene through contemporary art. Through video, installation, live art, documents, material experiments, and socially engaged art actions, artists re-weave spatial traces, labour memory, local experience, and contemporary perception within the salt industry heritage.

The exhibition is divided into two main sections: the Ziliujing Old Street Bookshop will present The Salt of Life Zigong Salt Culture Documentary Exhibition and selected works, unfolding around research-based art, project investigation, historical threads, local narratives, and public materials; Venue 2 of Ziliujing Old Street will concentrate on The Salt of Life Salt Culture Residency Outcomes Exhibition, with works distributed across the exhibition hall, corridors, outdoor public areas, terraces, and spaces facing the river, allowing artworks to enter into relationship with the local landscape of Ziliujing Old Street.

On the opening day, an opening ceremony, guided tour, and live art presentation will be held. Some resident artists, project team members, academic observers, urban observers, and guests from local and international art, culture, and research fields will come together on site to engage in public dialogue around Zigong salt culture, industrial heritage, and contemporary art practice.

The Salt of Life is a residency outcomes exhibition, a research documentary exhibition, and an art project intervening in the city about how Zigong salt culture can be re-perceived, re-told, and regenerated. It attempts to re-understand the cultural connotations of Zigong through art, and to write a new urban cultural story in old streets, communities, and public spaces.

About Diagonal / 关于对角线计划

Diagonal originated from bodily movement from northeast to southwest, forming a corresponding line from the “grassland, forest plain” to the “mountain highland” area on the Chinese map, echoing Hu Huanyong’s line from Heihe in the northeast to Tengchong in the southwest. From physical geography to human economy, from nearly a hundred years ago to contemporary society, from agricultural tradition to industrial prosperity, we try to find and relate common images, symbols, ideas and spirits, presenting the diverse features of the two regions in the form of art, combining practice and theory, and trying to summarise effective methods to promote and influence the evolution of local ecology.

The project is presented in diverse forms, including but not limited to sharing sessions, art festivals, exhibitions, workshops, field investigations, residencies, and publications. Diagonal currently has offline space clusters in Zigong City, Sichuan Province (Huojingtuo Creators’ Dormitory, SaltArt Space, Carbon Studio, etc.), and maintains long-term cooperative relationships with numerous domestic and international institutions and spaces.
