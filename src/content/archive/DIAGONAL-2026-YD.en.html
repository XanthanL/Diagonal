Retracing the Salt Road 2026: Walking Through History with Salt in Gongjing Old Street

Location: ZG / Zigong

In March 2026, we found ourselves in Gongjing Old Street, Zigong, attempting to use “salt” as a medium to re-measure this road across history. Salt is not only a mineral or an industry, but also a substance bearing the city’s collective memory and bodily perception.

In this action, we retraced the ancient salt road — not merely as physical movement, but as a re-encounter with historical texture. The tactile sensation of salt in hand and in motion re-embodied the ancient salt narrative within contemporary life.

Gongjing Old Street: Layers of Time and Matter

As a core area of the “Ziliujing Heritage Site,” Gongjing Old Street carries traces of centuries of salt industry operation. Its buildings and alleyways record the rise and fall of salt merchants and the labour of salt transport workers. In the present moment of 2026, we scattered salt anew upon these old sites, establishing a fresh dialogue with space through this embodied action.

This walking carried a certain ritual quality. By laying salt on the ground, we sought to materialise the concept of the “salt road,” allowing passers-by to re-perceive the weight of the land beneath their feet.

Reflection and Resonance

When salt once again becomes the object of action, it is no longer a static commodity but a connecting point of urban memory. Each step taken by participants is a retrospection of the past and a present record of the future.

Project Information

This is a key place-based action of The Salt of Life residency project. Through the combination of bodily language and material, it deeply explores the emerging relationship between industrial heritage and everyday urban life.
