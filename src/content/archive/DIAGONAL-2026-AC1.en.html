Academic Observers | 学术观察员

Jannis Chen | 陈济舟

Assistant Professor in the Department of Chinese Language and Literature at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, specialising in literary criticism with occasional fiction writing. Research interests include modern Sinophone literature, media and culture, borderland studies (southwest China), disaster culture and narrative, and thing theory. Has published academic papers in Modern Chinese Literature and Culture (MCLC), Journal of Modern Literature in Chinese, and Nanyang Journal, among others. Author of the short-story collection Tales of Yongfa Street; creative writings have appeared across mainland China, Hong Kong, Taiwan, Singapore, and Malaysia.

Tim Deng | 邓绍宏

PhD from the Department of East Asian Languages and Civilizations at Harvard University, currently Assistant Professor in the Department of Chinese Language and Literature at The Chinese University of Hong Kong. His research attends to ecology, technology, and media in the Sinophone world. His ongoing English monograph Binding Media: Geological Entanglements in Sinophone Extraction Zones adopts a resource-extractivism perspective, employing interdisciplinary methods to examine the dynamic relationships between environment and technology, image and politics across four historical extraction zones. Other research interests include media theory, media archaeology, East Asian cinema and visual culture, science and technology studies, and critical theory. His academic papers on East Asian media culture have appeared in international journals spanning film and media studies, literature and cultural studies, East Asian studies, human geography, geology, and other fields. He has also collaborated with The Museum of Modern Art (MoMA) in New York and the Harvard Film Archive to curate Chinese-language film festival sections.

Yifei Ren | 任一飞

Faculty member in the Department of Cross-Media Art at Xi'an Academy of Fine Arts, and executive curator of the Guanzhong Mangba Art Festival. His research mainly focuses on social art and participatory art, attending to the generation and efficacy of art practices within social space. He has initiated several community art projects locally, attempting to revitalise community life through rooted community engagement and collaborative participation, uncovering community issues, and rediscovering spaces of affective value within communities marked by mobility. He is also one of the initiators of Xi'an Co-Governance Space.

Nanming Wang | 王南溟

Artist, curator, and critic; former director of the Shanghai Himalayas Museum. In 2018, he used his concept of "More Avant-Garde Art" to create the "Community Hub Station", a new form of art museum, advancing the theory and practice of art communities. He has edited and published Art Communities in Shanghai: Cases and Forums and Crossing the Countryside: Art Communities and Sociological Art Festivals.

Yanhua Zhou | 周彦华

Art historian based in Atlanta, USA. After receiving her PhD in East Asian Studies from the University of Arizona, she served as Professor of Art Theory and doctoral supervisor at Sichuan Fine Arts Institute, Visiting Professor in the Department of East Asian Studies at the University of Arizona, and Visiting Researcher at Osaka University, Japan. In August 2025, she joined Kennesaw State University in Georgia, USA, where she is currently a tenure-track Assistant Professor of Asian Studies in the Department of World Languages and Cultures.

Her research takes socially engaged art as its point of departure, expanding to modern and contemporary art history and theory in China and the West, the history and theory of art's social intervention from the perspective of cultural pluralism, affect and public emotion theory, and regional/national art history under the concept of "Global Asia." Her current research focuses on infrastructure studies of research-based art, examining how, during China's era of massive infrastructure construction, art and curatorial practices respond to and reconsider the development, transformation, and overseas expansion of Chinese infrastructure through project-based research art.

Representative works include the monograph Art's Intervention: The Aesthetic Meaning-Generation Mechanism of Interventionist Art, the edited volume Art in Action: Discursive Practices of Socially Engaged Art in 21st-Century China, and the translation October: The Second Decade 1986–1996, among others. She is currently writing two monographs: the first discusses socialist art intervention and its Sinification, while the second examines art, curating, and place-remaking in China's "infrastructure mania" era.

Local Partner Spaces & Organisations | 本地合作活动空间 & 机构

Ziliujing Old Street | 自流井老街

Ziliujing Old Street is located in Guojia'ao Subdistrict, Ziliujing District, Zigong City, Sichuan Province, along the banks of the Fuxi River, and is an important historical and cultural block of Zigong. About 1.5 kilometres long, the old street nestles between mountains and water. It was once an ancient salt-transport route for well salt and is the site of the world-renowned "Ziliujing Well Ruins". As the birthplace of Zigong's well-salt culture, the old street carries the historical memory of the millennium-old salt capital and bears witness to the rise and fall of Zigong's salt industry and the city's development. Ziliujing Old Street has a long history, with its prosperity beginning in the Ming and Qing dynasties as an important component of the Furong Salt Fields. The block still preserves salt and gas well ruins such as Lubian Well and Qianchuan Well, as well as historical relics including Shanhoushi Bridge and the Salt-Transport Wharf. The architecture is primarily late-Qing and early-Republican southern Sichuan vernacular dwellings, preserving the traditional "shop in front, residence behind" pattern, with both undulating ordinary dwellings and deep-courtyard salt-merchant mansions. In recent years, Ziliujing Old Street has undergone protective restoration and renovation, becoming an open block integrating cultural tourism, leisure and entertainment, and distinctive commerce. In 2021, the old street was named a "Sichuan Provincial Tourism and Leisure Block" and a "Sichuan Provincial Pilot Pedestrian Street for Renovation and Upgrade". Today, the old street has not only restored its former salt-transport character but also incorporated modern cultural, commercial, and tourism elements, becoming an important window showcasing Zigong's salt culture and urban spirit.

Zigong Library | 自贡市图书馆

Zigong Library was founded in 1959; the current building was completed and opened in September 1996, located at 614 Huidong Road, Ziliujing District, Zigong. It has been recognised as a Sichuan Provincial Civilised Unit, a Sichuan Provincial Key Ancient Books Protection Unit, and a National First-Class Library. As an important site for public cultural service in the city, it persists in being demand-driven, upholding the principle of "Readers First, Service Foremost", and continuously optimising reading-resource allocation and service delivery so that citizens may enjoy a fuller, richer, and higher-quality reading and leisure life.

China Salt Culture Research Center, Sichuan University of Science & Engineering
四川轻化工大学 · 中国盐文化研究中心

The China Salt Culture Research Center is a provincial-level research institution jointly established by Sichuan University of Science & Engineering, the Zigong Salt Industry History Museum, and Sichuan Jiuda Salt Industry (Group), embodying the deep integration of "industry–academia–research". Since being named a Sichuan Provincial Key Humanities and Social Sciences Research Base for Higher Education in 2005, the centre has received numerous honours, including recognition as a Sichuan Provincial Key Philosophy and Social Sciences Research Base in 2007 and the Sichuan Provincial Government's "Golden Panda Award" in 2019. Currently, the centre is directed by Vice President Gao Xiaolin, with Zhang Qiang of the History Department in the School of Humanities serving as deputy director, jointly overseeing daily operations and academic planning.

Grounded in Zigong's deep historical and cultural heritage as the millennium-old salt capital, the centre strengthens the deep integration of regional culture and academic research; it is committed to the transmission and promotion of salt culture, facilitating the popularisation and conversion of research outcomes into society; and it advances the accumulation of academic achievements and theoretical construction, building a scholarly brand in salt culture research.

Liziran Art Space | 李子染艺术空间

The Liziran Tie-Dye Art Space is an innovative cultural-tourism display and experience base centred on Zigong tie-dye, a national-level intangible cultural heritage. The space integrates tie-dye exhibitions, ecological cultivation of dye plants, study-and-practice education, and group cultural activities, committed to the transmission, display, and living utilisation of intangible cultural heritage.

The space's director, Ms. Li Juan, has worked in the tie-dye craft for thirty-five years and holds multiple identities including Zigong Tie-Dye Intangible Cultural Heritage Inheritor, National-Level Rural Craftsman Master, Sichuan Provincial Master of Arts and Crafts, and Senior Arts and Crafts Master, infusing the operation with deep professional expertise.

Academic Guidance Body | 学术指导单位

Sichuan Alumni Association of the Sichuan Fine Arts Institute
四川美术学院四川校友会

The Sichuan Alumni Association of the Sichuan Fine Arts Institute is a non-profit social welfare organisation approved by the institute and officially established in 2024. Upholding the mission of "Three Pursuits, Three Establishments, Five Transmissions", the alumni association centres on continuing the Sichuan Fine Arts Institute's cultural lineage and carrying forward its spirit, aiming to build an alumni-gathering camp within the Chengdu-Chongqing dual-city economic circle and foster communication and cooperation between alumni and their alma mater.
