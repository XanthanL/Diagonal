Exhibition Review: The Salt of Life 1.0 — Zigong Salt Culture Research & Creation Residency Outcomes

Location: ZG / Zigong

From 14 March to 13 April 2026, The Salt of Life 1.0 — Zigong Salt Culture Research & Creation Residency Outcomes Exhibition was held at SaltArt Space and Lǎo Studio in the Huojingtuo area of Zigong. The exhibition was not only a concentrated presentation of the previous residency phase, but also a key step in bringing artistic practice into the community and exploring the possibilities of place-based culture and public narrative.

Exhibition Context: Huojingtuo Area

As an important industrial heritage area in Zigong’s salt industry history, Huojingtuo preserves not only unique architectural forms but also the memory of salt workers’ lives. The exhibition scattered works among old alleys, industrial remnants, and art spaces, allowing visitors to feel the flow of space and history.

Creation and Presentation

The exhibition presented the creative outcomes of resident artists over 20 days. From video documentation and oral history to spatial installations built from salt and found objects, participants attempted to materialise the abstract salt narrative as individual expression from a contemporary perspective.

The exhibition was not a static finished product, but a flowing discussion site. During the exhibition period, we organised multiple exchange sessions, inviting local residents, history researchers, and creators to participate together, turning the exhibition into an opportunity for community dialogue.

Significance and Continuation

This outcomes exhibition was not only a summary of the residency phase, but also laid a place-based foundation for the future deepening of The Salt of Life series in Zigong. Through this form, we are not only displaying art, but also constructing a sustainable dialogue mechanism that allows “salt” — as a substance — to continue growing between the city and the community.
