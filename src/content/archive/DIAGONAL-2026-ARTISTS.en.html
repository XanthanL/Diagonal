策展人前言 Exhibition Introduction

“生命之盐”：地景，复调，余味
Landscapes, Polyphony, Aftertaste

策展人：孙晓鸣 Kerribin

An exhibition is a performance of a residency journey, and also a contemplation of self-discovery through creation.

As the four-month Huojingtuo research and residency programme in Zigong draws to a close, in this summer that calls forth renewal in a southern Sichuan historical and cultural place saturated with the flavours of salt, spice, heat, and humidity, and half a year after the travelling creators entered into dialogue between the strangeness of the millennia of heavy labour etched into the Salt Capital and the familiar grains repeatedly crystallised by their own bodies, we present a feast of art that manifests our diverse emotions and temporal imaginations of this city.

Locality is a perceptual vocabulary that artists continually confront in their creative process; it also resonates with the buzzword that has frequently appeared in recent years — local knowledge. First-time artists arrive carrying indirect knowledge and enter this mountain city together with their senses. In the process of investigation, their semantic cells synthesise continuously with the direct experience of local guides, forming new place-based cognition and outputting later artistic "individual local imagery". Landscape here refers to the unique image of Zigong in each person's mind — it is both geographical and humanistic. In the obvious sensory overwriting narrative of the Salt Capital, the "Castle in the Sky" seems distant and fascinating; its series of enormous limbs occupy the visual centre intrusively, yet leave behind marginal after-images no longer inhabited. Artists attempt to record these vanishing stories through sound, feeling a different kind of story-writing from another world. We hear the breathing of some underground gas, almost unrelated to the images anchored by the hawking cries above ground, which is the creative material more commonly appropriated by artists. Besides this, the everyday we hear seems to fill the entire cranium like polyphony, and at the moment when sensory materials are stirred to the extreme, pungency is used as the finishing touch of this cooking. Yet artists have their own kingdoms of taste; they have already encountered numerous unexpected people, events, and things, digested these finely, and ultimately released the myriad artistic aftertastes of this place.

展览简述 Exhibition Overview

“生命之盐”

发起人：彭玮雯 Vivienne Peng

"Salt" seems to be the starting point and anchor of local life; here, salt is both material and memory, and is also integrated into the everyday. It connects Zigong's industrial history and enters today's modern life, bodily experience, and public space.

On 27 June 2026, "The Salt of Life" Contemporary Art Exhibition on Zigong Salt Culture will open at Ziliujing Old Street. This exhibition presents the phased results of the two phases of Diagonal's "The Salt of Life" Zigong Salt Culture Research and Creation Residency Programme 1.0 and 2.0. Focusing on contemporary art works, it includes installations, photography, video, live art, documentary displays, and local material experiments, with approximately 26 works/groups. The exhibition centrally presents the achievements of research, investigation, creation, and public activities conducted in the first half of 2026 around Zigong salt culture, industrial heritage, urban transformation, and community memory.

As a phased art project by Diagonal focusing on Zigong salt culture, this exhibition brings together more than twenty artists, researchers, and creators from different regions and backgrounds. Taking Zigong's urban salt culture as an index, we enter salt industry sites, old street spaces, industrial communities, markets, the Fuxi River banks, and everyday life scenes, conducting research, investigation, creation, and exchange around salt culture development, industrial heritage, urban transformation, and community memory.

"The Salt of Life" is not merely a project about the cultural history of salt, but a practice of re-entering the contemporary cultural scene of a place through contemporary art. Through video, installation, live art, documentation, material experiments, and socially engaged art actions, artists re-weave the spatial traces, labour memories, local experiences, and contemporary perceptions within salt industry heritage.

The exhibition is divided into two main sections: Ziliujing Old Street Bookshop will present "The Salt of Life" Zigong Salt Culture Documentary Exhibition and some works, focusing on research-based artworks, project research, historical clues, local narratives, and public materials; Ziliujing Old Street No. 2 Hall will concentrate on "The Salt of Life" Salt Culture Residency Achievement Exhibition, with works distributed across the exhibition hall, corridors, outdoor public areas, terraces, and spaces facing the river, allowing artworks to engage with the local landscape of Ziliujing Old Street.

On the opening day, there will be opening speeches, guided tours, and live art presentations. Some resident artists, project team members, academic observers, urban observers, and guests from local and domestic/international art, culture, and research fields will come together to engage in public dialogue on Zigong salt culture, industrial heritage, and contemporary art practice.

"The Salt of Life" is both a residency achievement exhibition and a research documentary exhibition, and even more an art project about how Zigong salt culture can be re-perceived, re-told, and regenerated. It attempts, through art, to re-understand the cultural connotation of Zigong and, in old streets, communities, and public spaces, write a new urban cultural story.

关于对角线计划 About Diagonal

The Diagonal originated from bodily movement from northeast to southwest, forming a corresponding line from the “grassland, forest plain” to the “mountain highland” area on the Chinese map, echoing Hu Huanyong’s line from Heihe in the northeast to Tengchong in the southwest. From physical geography to human economy, from nearly a hundred years ago to contemporary society, from agricultural tradition to industrial prosperity, we try to find and relate common images, symbols, ideas and spirits, presenting the diverse features of the two regions in the form of art, combining practice and theory, and trying to summarize effective methods to promote and influence the evolution of local ecology.

The project is presented in diverse forms, including but not limited to sharing sessions, art festivals, exhibitions, workshops, field investigations, residencies, and publications. The Diagonal currently has art spaces in Zigong, Sichuan Province (Huojingtuo Creators’ Dormitory, SaltArt Space, etc.), and maintains long-term cooperative relationships with numerous domestic and international institutions and spaces. The Diagonal has carried out research and contemporary art creation on the themes of industry, cities, ice and snow, and national culture in Hegang and Harbin in Heilongjiang Province, Shenyang and Fushun in Liaoning Province in northeast China, Chengdu, Zigong, Panzhihua, Liangshan Yi Autonomous Prefecture in Sichuan Province, Bijie in Guizhou Province, and Chongqing in southwest China.
