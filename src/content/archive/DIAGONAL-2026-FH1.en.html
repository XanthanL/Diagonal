DIAGONAL_SHARING_SESSION_REVIEW / 2026.07

Sharing Session Review | "The Salt of Life" Contemporary Art Exhibition on Zigong Salt Culture Sharing Session at Zigong City Library
分享会回顾｜"生命之盐"自贡盐文化当代艺术展分享会在自贡市图书馆举行

Location: ZG / Zigong

At 14:00 on 28 June 2026, the sharing session of "The Salt of Life" Contemporary Art Exhibition on Zigong Salt Culture was held in the fourth-floor lecture hall of the Zigong City Library.

As a continuation of the exhibition's opening programme, this sharing session was hosted by the curator and initiator of the Diagonal Project, Kerribin (Sun Xiaoming). Huang Yu, Director of the Zigong City Library; project initiator Vivienne Peng (Peng Weiwen); resident artists Christian, Jinglin, Liu Shengyang and Lu Rui (Cuicui); academic observer of this project, Professor Tim Deng (Deng Shaohong) of the Chinese University of Hong Kong; invited guest Professor Kevin (Liu Ziyu) of Guizhou University; and local Zigong culture researcher Yang Yuan; as well as Hudie (Madame Butterfly), founder and CEO of Yuxilong, took part in the sharing and discussion in succession. Other attendees included exhibition designer Lannie (Lan Yajie), the renowned contemporary artist Ni Weihua, Ma Song, Vice President of the Zigong Urban Planning & Design Institute, and Jing Yi, head of the Ziliujing Old Street operations team, among others.

The sharing session unfolded across project review, artists' residency experience, the process of work creation, resource extraction and media-cultural research, the Third Front construction infrastructure network, and Zigong local knowledge — bringing "The Salt of Life" from the exhibition space further into a public discussion site interweaving art, academia and local culture.

From the Exhibition Site into Public Discussion | 从展览现场进入公共讨论

"The Salt of Life" Contemporary Art Exhibition on Zigong Salt Culture opened at Ziliujing Old Street on 27 June, presenting the phased achievements of the two phases of the Diagonal Project's "Salt of Life" research-creation residency programme 1.0 and 2.0, alongside "The Salt of Life" Zigong Salt Culture Documentary Exhibition, co-produced with the China Salt Culture Research Center, which also opened to the public in the same period.

Since February 2026, artists, researchers and creators from different regions and backgrounds have successively entered the urban site of Zigong, conducting investigation, interviews, material experiments, art creation and socially engaged actions across salt-industry historical sites, old streets, industrial communities, wet markets, riverbanks and public spaces.

This project also consistently embodies the "long-termism" upheld by the Diagonal Project. As the first appearance of a Zigong urban cultural art project, we attend to salt culture, industrial heritage, urban transformation and community memory, while also attempting to establish a locally operable, long-term art-practice mechanism.

It is on this basis that this sharing session brings the works, processes and questions of the exhibition back into discussion.

Sharing Session Programme Introduction and Addresses | 分享会流程介绍与致辞

The sharing session was hosted by Kerribin (Sun Xiaoming), curator of this exhibition and initiator of the Diagonal Project.

Kerribin has long been engaged in art-project curation, local cultural research and public cultural practice, and has continually promoted the Diagonal Project's cross-regional investigation, residency and art exchange between northeastern and southwestern China. At the start of the event, he introduced the guests, the topics and the overall programme to the audience.

Then, Huang Yu, Director of the Zigong City Library, delivered an address on behalf of the partner organisation. She welcomed the artists, researchers and audience participating in this exhibition and sharing session, and from the perspective of a public cultural institution responded to the possibility of building connections among local archives, urban memory and contemporary art practice.

From the children's socially engaged art project "Echoes of the Land" to this "Salt of Life" exhibition sharing session, the Zigong City Library has collaborated with the Diagonal Project on multiple occasions. Ms. Huang Yu fully affirmed the significance of art projects entering urban public cultural space, holding that a library is not only a place for preserving knowledge and providing reading services, but can also become a platform where art practice, public education and social exchange continue to occur. She also welcomed more high-quality cultural and art projects from different fields to enter the Zigong City Library, providing the public with more open, accessible and diverse opportunities for cultural participation and learning.

From the "Salt of Life 1.0" Residency Programme to Long-Term Place-Based Practice | 从"生命之盐1.0"驻留计划到长期在地实践

After the addresses, project initiator Vivienne Peng (Peng Weiwen) gave a systematic account of the project's development, practical methods and phased achievements, under the title "The Salt of Life: Review of the Contemporary Art Exhibition on Zigong Salt Culture and the Research-Creation Residency Programme".

Vivienne Peng is a co-initiator of the Diagonal Project, a PhD candidate in Codex at Plymouth University (UK) and a jointly supervised doctoral candidate at Soochow University. Her long-term research and art practice centres on industrial heritage, material memory, socially engaged art and post-work-unit communities.

She began by introducing the formation of the Diagonal Project. The Diagonal Project originated in bodily movement from northeast to southwest China, attempting to establish connections across the natural geography, industrial history, urban experience and social life of different regions, and to continually advance cross-regional art research and practice through residency, investigation, exhibition, live art, workshops, public discussion and publication.

Vivienne Peng then introduced in detail the resident artists, invited artists, curatorial and executive team, academic observers, urban observers, on-site staff and volunteers who took part in the "Salt of Life" series, and expressed gratitude to the partner institutions, local cultural spaces and community participants who have long supported the project. She noted that "The Salt of Life" was not accomplished by a single artist or curatorial team, but gradually generated through the joint participation of artists, researchers, local cultural workers, community residents, public cultural institutions and collaborators from different professional fields. Participants with different identities and knowledge backgrounds brought to the project multiple perspectives on salt-industry history, industrial heritage, local life, bodily experience and public culture, and turned residency, creation, exhibition and discussion into an interlinked practice network.

She then introduced the spatial foundation the project has gradually built in Zigong, including SaltArt Space, Carbon Studio and Huojingtuo Creators' Dormitory. These spaces respectively host public display, material experimentation, artwork creation, communal living and discussion, so that residency is no longer merely an artist's short-term visit to a city, but becomes a process in which participants build relationships, share experience, conduct joint research and collaborate in creation.

"The Salt of Life 1.0" was conducted intensively from February to April 2026. Participants entered Zigong through salt-industry historical tracing, industrial-heritage investigation, community research, communal living and material experimentation.

"The Salt of Life 2.0" then continued through June in a rolling residency format, providing artists with more flexible time for investigation, research and creation. The two phases both continue one another and, according to the directions of participants' practices, form different working rhythms.

The project successively visited the Salt Industry History Museum, Xiqin Guild Hall, Shenhai Well, Old Salt Works 1957, Ziliujing Old Street, Gongjing Old Street, Xianshi Ancient Town, Aiye Ancient Town, Jiuda Salt Industry Heritage Site, Ziliujing Salt Factory Heritage Site, Guojiacao Market and the Dongxin Carbon Factory community, among other places. Through walking, observation, interviews and image recording, artists entered salt-production sites, industrial communities and the everyday city. In this process, the city was no longer merely the backdrop for the works, but became an important research site for understanding salt-industry history, industrial transformation, community structure and local life.

Vivienne Peng also reviewed a series of activities including the "Rewalking the Salt Road" socially engaged art action, "Salt Exchange", on-site salt selling and online livestream, the "Salt of the Deep Well" art therapy workshop, internal artist work-sharing sessions and "Diagonal Conversations". These practices connect bodily walking, local memory, commodity exchange, public dialogue and art creation, turning salt from a static exhibition material into a medium that links people, history, space and social relations.

At the end of her sharing, she proposed that "The Salt of Life" does not take a single exhibition as the project's endpoint. The exhibition is both a concentrated presentation of phased achievements and an important node for re-examining practical methods, relationships of participation and local responsibility. How to turn short-term residency into sustained cooperation, how to make art research genuinely respond to concrete and complex local experience, and how to maintain already-established relationships after the project ends — these remain questions that "The Salt of Life" must continually face and consider in future practice.

Artist Sharing: From Residency Experience Back into the Work | 艺术家分享：从驻留经验回到作品内部

Following the project review, Christian, Jinglin, Liu Shengyang and Lu Rui (Cuicui) each shared their experiences during the Zigong residency, and the creative process of their works — from investigation and perception to final presentation.

Christian | Time, Pickling and the Memory of Repeated Trips to Zigong

Christian reviewed her observations during the residency on time, everyday materials and the transformation of memory.

Her work Periods uses pickle jars, flowers, salt, water, UV resin and video to place the experience of multiple trips to Zigong within a process akin to pickling and preservation. The materials inside the jars change over time, pointing both to familiar everyday storage methods and to the unstable state of memory in retention, sedimentation and transformation.

In her sharing, residency is not a linear process from entering a place to completing a work, but is constituted by repeated arrivals, departures, waitings and re-viewings. Time is not only the background of the work but directly participates in the work's generation.

Jinglin | Finding "Evidence of Existence" Between Matter, Spirit and Space

Jinglin departed from her long-standing concerns with expressive art, neurodiversity, the flow of power in relationships and the materialisation of spiritual experience, and reviewed the formation of her work The SUNs.

The work uses fishing line, mirrors, clay, salt and gilded glass gravel to form relations of suspension, refraction and connection in space. Salt, light and fragments are not purely formal materials but media through which the artist understands spiritual perception, spatial bearing capacity and individual existence.

Jinglin's sharing also responded to the distance between language and experience during the residency. When a creator enters a place not entirely familiar, the work need not immediately offer a clear explanation; it can also preserve perception, contradiction and what cannot be fully captured by language.

Liu Shengyang | How the Body Enters the Environment of a Salt-Industry City

Creator and dancer Liu Shengyang, taking the body as medium, shared the creation process of the environmental body theatre work Realm. The work plan presented in Realm reflects the artist's serious exploration during her time on site, integrating concept and body into a way of sensing local culture.

In her sharing, Liu Shengyang reviewed her learning experience from folk dance and Chinese classical dance to modern dance and bodily creation, and discussed how, starting from personal bodily sensation, she came to understand the extraction and flow of salt and the historical traces within urban space.

The final live work unfolded at the Water Theatre of Ziliujing Old Street, where the body jointly composed the performance environment together with the river, bridge, architecture, light, sound and the live audience. Creation is not the transplantation of pre-completed movements to a new site, but the sensing, in a concrete space, of how the body is transformed by the environment and how it responds to the environment through movement.

Lu Rui (Cuicui) | Re-recognising "Flavour" Within a Familiar Hometown

Lu Rui (Cuicui) is a Zigong-based creator. Starting from her personal growth, local life and subtle sensations, she shared the creative review of her work Flavour.

Flavour took place at the end of the corridor in Venue 2 of Ziliujing Old Street, with thread, bells, salt, chairs and the body composing the live site. The work extends taste from a direct sensation on the tip of the tongue to bodily memory, emotional experience and the complex relationship between person and place.

For someone born in Zigong, salt culture is both familiar and easily obscured by everyday life. Re-entering one's hometown does not mean naturally possessing complete local knowledge. Residency and creation instead make certain taken-for-granted experiences newly unfamiliar, and lead the creator to begin discerning the emotions between herself and the city that resist simple summary.

Her sharing makes "locality" no longer only a question faced by visiting artists entering Zigong, but also a process through which local creators re-view their hometown, family and self-experience.

Tim Deng: Excavation, Binding and Weaving — Entering Extraction-Zone Research from Zigong | 邓绍宏：挖掘、捆绑与编织——从自贡进入采掘区研究

After the artist sharing, academic observer of this project, Tim Deng (Deng Shaohong), Assistant Professor in the Department of Chinese Language and Literature at the Chinese University of Hong Kong, shared his cultural research on Zigong and Sinophone extraction zones, taking "excavation, binding and weaving" as his thread.

Tim Deng holds a PhD from the Department of East Asian Languages and Civilizations at Harvard University, and his research attends to ecology, technology and media in the Sinophone world. The English monograph he is currently writing, Binding the Land: Media and the Geological Entanglement of Sinophone Extraction Zones, proceeds from the perspective of resource extractivism to explore the dynamic relations between environment and technology, image and politics within historical extraction zones.

In this sharing, he indicated that "excavation" refers not only to the technical action of extracting brine from underground, but also to the geology, time and labour reorganised by industrial production; "binding" signifies that land, resources, equipment, institutions, images, bodies and local society are not isolated from one another but continually connected through the extraction process; "weaving" reminds us that the history of a resource-based city is co-constituted by numerous materials, technologies and social relations.

Zigong's salt wells, brine pipelines, production equipment, urban architecture, workers' communities and visual archives can thereby be understood as mutually entangled media systems. Geology is not a silent natural backdrop but an active force that continually participates in urban formation, technological development and social organisation.

This perspective also offers a new research entry for "The Salt of Life": what artists face is not only a material called "salt", but the land, technology, labour, capital, images, families and local memory connected by salt across its long history of extraction.

Kevin (Liu Ziyu): Third Front Construction and the Relational Network of Infrastructure | 刘子瑜：三线建设与基础设施的关系网络

Dr. Kevin (Liu Ziyu) approached from Third Front construction research to share the relational network of infrastructure.

Kevin is currently a university-appointed Associate Professor and master's supervisor at Guizhou University. He graduated from the Department of Communication Studies at the University of Minnesota (USA) with a PhD in philosophy, and is a member of the International Communication Association, the International Association for Media and Communication Research, and the Association for Asian Studies, as well as a member of the International Communication Committee of the Chinese Journalism History Association.

His sharing did not directly address the history and culture of "salt", but instead began with another landmark event in modern Zigong — Third Front construction — moving outward from a single factory or industrial history toward an overall observation of infrastructure systems. Taking Third Front factories in Guizhou as an example, he showed that industrial production is not accomplished by a single isolated historic building but depends on multiple conditions: roads, transport, energy, communications, residential space, institutional organisation and population migration. Especially within data-centre networks, the infrastructure network of Third Front construction forms a thread interweaving history, culture, economy and politics.

These infrastructures appear hidden and isolated in the mountains yet are in fact interlinked: on one hand they carry material transport and industrial production functions, and on the other they participate in shaping community relations, family life and local identity. As industrial transformation proceeds, some production facilities have gradually ceased operation, but the spatial structures, social relations and ways of life they established continue to influence the city today.

This discussion resonates with the project's investigations in places such as the Dongxin Carbon Factory community and the Ziliujing Salt Factory heritage site. When art enters an industrial community, what needs to be seen is not only the visible factory buildings and equipment, but also the relational network that supported their operation and endures to this day.

Yang Yuan: Response from Local History and Salt-Culture Experience | 杨源：从地方史与盐文化经验出发的回应

During the commentary and exchange session, Yang Yuan, specially appointed researcher at the China Salt Culture Research Center, responded to the artists' and scholars' sharing from the perspectives of Zigong local history, well-salt culture, archaeological heritage and folk collecting.

Yang Yuan is a Sichuan Provincial cultural-relics archaeological consultant, a member of the Zigong Local History Expert Database, Vice President of the Zigong Folk Literature and Art Association, and Vice President & Director of the Appraisal Committee of the Zigong Collectors Association, long engaged in research on Zigong local history, well-salt culture and folk documentation.

His remarks supplemented the sharing session with historical experience and a knowledge perspective from within the place. For Zigong, salt culture encompasses not only macro-level industrial history and urban-development history, but is also concretely present in local documents, production tools, folk collections, oral memory and everyday-life customs.

Yang Yuan also specifically responded to the historical materials concerning Gongjing, Ziliujing and other places in Professor Tim Deng's sharing, pointing to local experiences and historical perspectives in existing materials not yet sufficiently represented, and further recommended representative well-salt production areas such as Huojingtuo as important sites for subsequent investigation and research. These supplements, drawn from long-term local research and field experience, provide valuable clues for the project's further understanding of the relations among Zigong's salt-industry history, production spaces and local society, and offer important references for subsequent art practice and academic research.

In addition, Ms. Hudie, founder and CEO of the local food-industry brand Yuxilong, shared her experience of taking part in the art therapy and exhibition activities. As a local history and culture enthusiast, Ms. Hudie is active on self-media platforms under the name "Madame Butterfly". She looks forward to more contemporary art content taking root in Zigong and forming more wonderful connections with this city in the future.

From Work Sharing Toward an Encounter of Multiple Knowledges | 从作品分享走向多种知识的相遇

This sharing session was not a simple restatement of the exhibition or project content. When art creation and academic research enter a local site, on one hand they can propose new ways of seeing; on the other, they also need to establish deeper dialogue with existing local knowledge. The theoretical perspectives of visiting researchers, the sensory experience of resident artists and the long-accumulated documentary knowledge of local researchers complement one another in exchange, and also reveal differences among ways of producing knowledge.

From Vivienne Peng's systematic review of the two residency phases, to the four artists' accounts of their creative process and bodily experience, to Tim Deng's research on the geological entanglement of extraction zones, Kevin's analysis of the Third Front infrastructure network, and Yang Yuan's response grounded in Zigong local history, different scales of observation converged in the fourth-floor lecture hall of the Zigong City Library.

Artists' knowledge comes from the body, materials, walking, communal living and on-site practice; academic research re-understands resource-based cities through concepts, history and cross-regional comparison; local research is built upon documents, remains, oral tradition and long-term lived experience.

They do not need to reach a unified conclusion in a single discussion. It is precisely in difference, supplementation and mutual questioning that the history and reality connected by salt become more complex.

"The Salt of Life" thereby becomes not only an exhibition about salt culture, but an experiment in which multiple kinds of knowledge jointly enter a place: from underground resources to urban space, from industrial infrastructure to community life, from bodily perception to public memory, from individual creation to local cultural research.

"The Salt of Life" Continues | 持续中的"生命之盐"

After the sharing session, the exhibition remains open at Ziliujing Old Street, running from 27 June to 16 August 2026.

From SaltArt Space, Carbon Studio and Huojingtuo Creators' Dormitory, to the Ziliujing Old Street Bookshop and Venue 2; from the two residency phases, salt-road walks and community research, to the exhibition, live art and public sharing — "The Salt of Life" is gradually unfolding across different spaces and relations.

This is not a finished answer, but a long-term practice still growing.

Salt once connected a city through wells, pipelines, boats and roads. Today, it forms new relational networks through art, research, memory and human encounters.

Excavation continues
Binding keeps occurring
And new local stories are being rewoven
