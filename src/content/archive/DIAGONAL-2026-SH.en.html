DIAGONAL_LECTURE / 2026.06

Review: Diagonal at the Shanghai Community Hub Station Lecture

Location: SH / Shanghai

On 5 June 2026, Diagonal was invited to participate in the Shanghai Community Hub Station lecture themed “Art Community and Disciplinary Innovation.” This lecture represented an important dialogue for the project across different cultural and spatial contexts.

From Place-based Practice to Disciplinary Innovation

During the lecture, we reviewed Diagonal’s long-term cross-regional practice from northeast to southwest. By sharing community art museum projects in Hegang and Zigong, we explored how contemporary art can cross geographical boundaries and take root in different social contexts.

The lecture focused on the role of interdisciplinary collaboration in contemporary art community practice: art is no longer an isolated aesthetic object, but functions as a sociological method that establishes long-term mechanisms within communities through cross-disciplinary dialogue and cooperation.

The “Diagonal” from an Interdisciplinary Perspective

We emphasized that Diagonal is concerned not only with artworks themselves, but with a “mechanism of relationship generation.” Through an anthropological lens reflecting on social practice, artistic creation can break free from the traditional museum paradigm and reach the nerve endings of urban transformation.

About This Lecture

This lecture was a public summary and academic examination of the project from the perspective of contemporary disciplinary research.
