Opening Forum Review | The Salt of Life 1.0: Zigong Salt Culture Research & Creation Residency

Location: ZG / Zigong

On 5 March 2026, the opening forum of The Salt of Life 1.0: Zigong Salt Culture Research & Creation Residency, initiated by Diagonal, was successfully held at Fangting Bookstore on Ziliujing Old Street, Zigong. The forum was both a concentrated presentation of phase outcomes and a public discussion around salt industry history, community memory, and socially engaged art methodologies.

Artists, researchers, local participants, and the public from diverse backgrounds joined together, transforming the project — beyond the exhibition — into an open public site.

On-site: Ziliujing Old Street and Fangting Bookstore

Ziliujing Old Street, where the forum took place, is one of the important spatial remnants of Zigong’s salt industry history. It was once a core area where salt merchants gathered, well salt was transported, and urban life intertwined, and today it forms a unique “layering of time” between urban renewal and everyday life.

As a cultural node within the old street, Fangting Bookstore is not only a space for reading and exchange, but has gradually become a platform connecting local residents, cultural practitioners, and public discussion. Holding the forum here extended the conversation from the “exhibition space” into “urban everyday life,” allowing art practice to truly enter the context of living.

“Salt” as Medium: Entering the City and the Art Scene

The forum opened with a sharing session by Vivienne Peng, initiator of The Salt of Life project. Starting from the study of “material memory,” she explained how the project uses salt — a concrete material — as an entry point to connect industrial heritage, community experience, and contemporary art practice.

In his speech, Kerribin summarised the overall practice of Diagonal. He pointed out that the project is not only artistic creation, but also a mechanism of relationship generation, enabling participants to re-understand their connection with the city through action. He stressed that Zigong is not only an important salt industry city in China, but also an industrial heritage site still in transformation.

Residency Practice: Artists and Participants

The residency brought together multiple creators including Guan Jiefu, Hu Tengkai, Li Zengyu, Lin Ying, Liu Xingyu, lulu, Lu Kuang (Cuicui), Ma Yiming, Vivienne Peng, Seem, Xiaoku, and Xiaoyuer. Starting from the materiality of salt, they explored and reconstructed local memory through video, oral history, community interaction, and other diverse approaches.

At the same time, the project was co-constructed through multi-party collaboration, forming an open, fluid, and sustainable local cultural network. Special thanks to academic observers Jannis Chen and Zhou Yanhua, and to staff members Liu Hongwu, Peng Sichuang, Liao Jun, and Yang Chuanwei for their deep participation.

Place-based Practice: From Methodology to On-site Action

The forum systematically reviewed the overall process and methodological framework of the project:

  • Urban cultural investigation: Walking through the Salt Industry History Museum, Shenhai Well, the thousand-year salt road, and other sites, perceiving how salt industry history shaped urban structure and ways of life.
  • Community research: Through interviews and documentation, shifting the perspective from historical remnants to real community experience.
  • Material experiments: Salt, bamboo, video, found objects, and other materials were recombined here.
  • Exhibition presentation: Phase presentations at SaltArt Space and Lǎo Studio.

The “Retracing the Salt Road” action became an important case. Participants walked the ancient salt roads of Zigong with their bodies, making salt a medium connecting people, history, and space, so that history no longer remained in museum narratives.

Methodology of Socially Engaged Art

The forum explicitly proposed Socially Engaged Art (SEA) and Participatory Action Research (PAR) as methodological frameworks. The goal is not solely to “complete a work,” but to focus more on the generation of social action and the unfolding of process.

“The value of socially engaged art is often generated in these concrete and minute connections.”

Ongoing Project: Looking Forward

The Salt of Life 1.0 is a phase of beginning. In the future, Diagonal will continue to advance residency practice in Zigong, exploring how art practice can be transformed into a long-term social mechanism.
