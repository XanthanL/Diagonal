Opening Day Review | "The Salt of Life" Contemporary Art Exhibition on Zigong Salt Culture Opens at Ziliujing Old Street
开幕日回顾｜"生命之盐"自贡盐文化当代艺术展在自流井老街开幕

Location: ZG / Zigong

On 27 June 2026, "The Salt of Life" Contemporary Art Exhibition on Zigong Salt Culture officially opened at Ziliujing Old Street.

Initiated by the Diagonal Project, this exhibition jointly presents the phased achievements of the two phases of "The Salt of Life" Zigong Salt Culture Research and Creation Residency Programme 1.0 and 2.0, together with "The Salt of Life" Zigong Salt Culture Documentary Exhibition. Taking Zigong salt culture, industrial heritage, urban transformation and community memory as its threads, the exhibition gathers artists, researchers, creators and local participants from different regions and backgrounds across the world. Through painting, installation, photography, moving image, live art, web art, documentary display and local material experiments, it brings salt culture and contemporary art together for the first time at Ziliujing Old Street. Across the bookshop, exhibition halls, corridors, terraces, riverbanks and public spaces, it unfolds a contemporary art site concerned with salt, the body, memory and place-based experience.

Opening Speeches: Launching the Exhibition in the Bookshop Atrium of the Old Street | 开幕致辞：在老街书店中庭开启展览

At 16:30 on 27 June, the opening ceremony took place in the atrium of the Ziliujing Old Street Bookshop. Curator Kerribin (Sun Xiaoming) served as host, first introducing the basic information, project nature, exhibition concept, venues, partners and the long-term practical lineage of the Diagonal Project.

As the project initiator, Vivienne Peng (Peng Weiwen) spoke from the dual perspective of a local researcher and artist, sharing how "The Salt of Life" grew out of Zigong's salt-industry history, industrial-heritage communities, family memory and individual experience. She especially thanked the local friends, residents, partners and participants in Zigong who have supported the project along the way. It is precisely this trust, assistance and everyday connection from within the place that has made "The Salt of Life" not only an exhibition but also an ongoing place-based practice.

The curator Kerribin then introduced the project members present and those unable to attend, including the curatorial team, resident artists, invited artists, academic observers, urban observers, visual designers, spatial designers, exhibition executors, exhibition consultants, on-site staff and the installation team.

Resident artists of this exhibition: Group 52342 (Lesliecan & Fried Egg Tart), Beetwise (Liu Xinghao, Lulu, Xiaoyu), Christian, Guan Jeff, Hu Weizhao, JINAN (Wang Zinan), Jinglin, Li Jingming, Li Zengyu, Lin Liying, Linda Hong Cheng, Liu Shengyang, Lu Rui (Cuicui), Ma Yiming, Matt Parker, Seem, Vivienne Peng, Xiaoyuer, Yuxuan.

Invited artists: Bonsai Peng, InsectSound, Li Juan, Li Yongzheng, Yang Chuanwei, Lannie, Yang Qian, Peng Siwei.

Project team: curator Kerribin (Sun Xiaoming), curatorial assistant Christian, exhibition designer Lannie (Lan Yajie), exhibition executor Vivienne Peng (Peng Weiwen), exhibition consultant Yang Qian, visual designers Tofu (Liu Luofu) and Vivienne Peng.

Academic observers: Jannis Chen, Tim Deng (Deng Shaohong), Yifei Ren, Nanming Wang, Yanhua Zhou.

Urban observers: Hao Li (Li Hao), Hao Yang (Bai Gaozi), Xin Tong.

Installation team: Fugui Electrical Shop (Yang Chuanwei, Yang Liuchuan, Yang Hong), Li Peijing.

These participants, coming from different cities, professions and life experiences, together form the open and complex creative network of this exhibition.

Guest Speeches: Multiple Responses from Locality, Academy and Art | 嘉宾发言：地方、学术与艺术的多重回应

The opening ceremony also introduced the invited leaders and guests who spoke, including Huang Yu, Director of the Zigong City Library (the partner venue); academic observer of this exhibition, Professor Tim Deng (Deng Shaohong) of the Chinese University of Hong Kong; renowned contemporary artist Ni Weihua, who travelled specially from Shanghai; and Hu Shaozhong, President of the Sichuan Alumni Association of the Sichuan Fine Arts Institute.

In her speech, Ms. Huang Yu responded to the relationship between this exhibition and Zigong's public cultural spaces, local archives and urban memory. As the venue for the exhibition sharing session, the Zigong City Library has collaborated with the Diagonal Project on multiple occasions — for example, providing important public cultural support for the earlier children's socially engaged art project "Echoes of the Land" — and this time offered strong support by providing the sharing-session venue for "The Salt of Life". Director Huang expressed her recognition and encouragement for the contemporary art project on Zigong salt culture.

From the perspective of research and observation, Professor Tim Deng (Deng Shaohong) of the Chinese University of Hong Kong shared the practical significance of the project within a local cultural site. His speech situated "The Salt of Life" within broader local research, cultural memory and contemporary art practice, responding to how art can become a method for understanding urban experience.

From the perspective of a creator, contemporary artist Ni Weihua recalled his encounter with the Diagonal Project, drawing on the early experience of Shanghai's avant-garde art entering local contexts to reflect on the current state of the Zigong project. He expressed appreciation for how the project enters history, the public and the relations between people.

Finally, invited guest Hu Shaozhong, President of the Sichuan Alumni Association of the Sichuan Fine Arts Institute, expressed his support on site for the contemporary art practice that young Sichuan Fine Arts Institute alumni are carrying out in Zigong.

Exhibition Guided Tour: From the Bookshop to Venue 2, From Artworks to the Urban Site | 展览导览：从书店到2号馆，从作品到城市现场

After the opening speeches, the audience, led by the curator and project members, began to enter the exhibition site. The exhibition is divided into two main sections: Venue 1, the Ziliujing Old Street Bookshop (Hall 36), presents "The Salt of Life" Zigong Salt Culture Documentary Exhibition and some works, organised around research-based artworks, project investigation, historical threads, local narratives and public materials; Venue 2, Hall A02 of Ziliujing Old Street, concentrates on "The Salt of Life" Salt Culture Residency Achievement Exhibition, with works distributed across the exhibition hall, corridors, outdoor public areas, terraces and building spaces facing the river.

The guided tour began at the Ziliujing Old Street Bookshop (Hall 36), opening first with "The Salt of Life" Zigong Salt Culture Documentary Exhibition in the bookshop atrium — a collaboration between the Diagonal Project and the China Salt Culture Research Center of Sichuan University of Science & Engineering. It presents rarely seen archival materials and documents, including literature, historical records, painting collections, research journals and popular publications, embodying the academic and place-based research character of the project and its deep integration with the historical culture of the Ziliujing Old Street site.

Yang Qian | Old Street 2013, Base Area Teahouse

Presented in the atrium and the left-side exhibition hall of the bookshop section are the documentary Old Street 2013 and the photographic work Base Area Teahouse by Zigong-born artist Yang Qian. Taking Ziliujing Old Street as a thread of memory and starting from personal growth, the moving-image work re-examines the spatial changes and everyday life of the old street.

Matt Parker | Slow Records

Directly facing the entrance to the left-side exhibition hall is Slow Records by UK-based artist Matt Parker. The artist places Zigong salt, brine, Rochelle salt, glass jars, Petri dishes and cassette recorders side by side, allowing crystal growth and field recordings together to become a record of the slow deposition of time.

Li Yongzheng | Fuxi River

Fuxi River, by the renowned artist Li Yongzheng, follows the actual geographical course of Zigong's Fuxi River, cutting a river path running through the city out of salt blocks on the wall. It is both a geographical trace and a fissure in space, a cross-section of urban memory.

JINAN | Some Forgotten Fragmentary Moments, DidaDida...

JINAN (Wang Zinan), about to begin postgraduate study at the Central Academy of Fine Arts, created two bodies of work, Some Forgotten Fragmentary Moments and DidaDida..., using cloth, salt, ladders and sea-salt blocks to address the relationships among time, labour, memory and crystallisation. Trained in arts and crafts yet combining an experimental art language, the artist achieves a relatively perfect unity of concept and aesthetic expression.

Yuxuan | The Unique (Part One)

Yuxuan's The Unique (Part One) is an installation made of salt, wood, light bulbs, aluminium foil, acrylic and rope, juxtaposing the significance of salt in spiritual ritual and everyday cooking to explore the dissolution of binary oppositions. The artist uses abstraction to render an inner world; spirit and matter become mutually interpreting subjects here.

After viewing the works and documents in the two halls of the bookshop, the guided tour moved from the Ziliujing Old Street Bookshop (Hall 36) into Venue 2. Following the exhibition route, the audience encountered more than twenty works/groups across different spaces. Some start from the materiality of salt, some return to Zigong's old streets, rivers, markets and factory sites, some enter the depths of the body, family, childhood, taste and memory, while others — through digital archives, live actions and environmental body theatre — push the exhibition onward into public space.

Li Juan | Salt Element

On the exterior of Venue 2 is Salt Element by Li Juan, a Zigong tie-dye intangible-heritage inheritor. Three immense tie-dyed fabric panels hang down from the second-floor balcony. Salt Element combines the crystalline aesthetics of Zigong well salt with the national-level intangible heritage craft of tie-dye, using cotton cloth, plant indigo and Zigong tie-dye technique to present the forms and textures of salt, and extending into public space in close integration with the architecture of Ziliujing Old Street.

Lannie | Lantern Festival

Then, as everyone began entering Venue 2, immediately inside the right hall was Lantern Festival by Lannie. Using UV-printed PVC panels and LED strips, the artist constructed a light installation that attempts to recover her faded, fragmented childhood memories of the Zigong Lantern Festival.

Christian | Periods

To the left of the entrance to the right hall is Periods by Christian. Over nearly three months of residency, the artist used pickle jars, flowers, salt, water, UV resin and video to record repeated trips to Zigong. Pickling is like memory: it transforms some things and preserves others. Christian, present on site, also had lively exchanges with the audience about the making and concepts of contemporary floral art; the artist turned these floral pieces into small polaroid souvenirs displayed on site.

Beetwise | Zigong Photo Studio

Beetwise's Zigong Photo Studio departs from a worker's retirement certificate, gathering, reconstructing and re-viewing anonymous historical images from the collectivist era. The work consists of three parts: a photographic apparatus, inkjet photographs and cyanotype cloth. The images across the three media appear with varying degrees of blur; the author attempts through this visual experience to open up and activate the viewer's memories of Zigong.

Yuxuan | The Unique (Part Two)

In Yuxuan's The Unique (Part Two), alongside the installation made once again from salt, wood, light bulbs, aluminium foil, acrylic and rope, the artist continues to use painting to express the relationship between material salt and an abstract spiritual world, continually exploring new and old life experiences triggered by Zigong's symbols.

Bonsai Peng | Triple Eye

Artist Bonsai Peng's Triple Eye was filmed at the Futaishan Tunnel Market in Zigong and presented as a three-channel video installation juxtaposing the market's everyday life under different viewing mechanisms. Across the worlds of an automatic sensor camera, a DV camcorder used by market vendors, and a professional camera, we can further see and feel the distinctive social spectacle of the mountain city. Several stools are placed in front of the three players, with sunflower seeds and snacks on them — a direct embodiment of the artist's extension of a sense of the everyday.

After the guided tour of the right hall of Venue 2, the curator led everyone into the larger left hall.

Peng Siwei | Diagonal Project: Digital Documentary Archive System

Placed close to the entrance is Peng Siwei's Diagonal Project: Digital Documentary Archive System. As the engineering designer of the Diagonal Project's online website, he uses digital technology to build a project archive, organising long-term images, texts, actions and research materials into a continuously accessible online system. This website is both his work and the project's official external presentation system — a new conceptual form of "web art", an open interactive digital-media platform that connects domestic and international audiences and serves as an important vehicle for the future internationalisation and art-commerce dimensions of the Diagonal Project.

Jinglin | The SUNs

The SUNs was one of the most-circled works on opening day. As an art therapist, Jinglin uses fishing line, mirrors, clay, salt and gilded glass gravel to construct a spatial installation that tries to address a question: "Where do human beings encounter the sun?" The artist made the work on site with more than two hundred lines; this process of artistic production unfolded alongside our installation process — a particular feature of the residency project.

Yang Chuanwei | Vacuum Salt-Making Model

Yang Chuanwei is a member of this exhibition's installation team and also a creator versed in all kinds of mechanical and electronic handwork. His installation Vacuum Salt-Making Model is based on an old model blueprint; in his spare time he turned the drawing on paper into a real, scaled-down model — "real" in the sense that it actually runs. Against the current wave of art-and-technology creation, Yang Chuanwei offers another approach and identity: to artisticise genuine scientific products, rather than simply grafting scientific principles onto artistic language.

Hu Weizhao & Li Zengyu | I Must Find the Salt Factory in One Day

I Must Find the Salt Factory in One Day is a joint work by Hu Weizhao of the Shanghai Academy of Fine Arts, Shanghai University, and Li Zengyu of Fudan University, both doctoral candidates. Created during the "Salt of Life" 1.0 phase, it is a multimedia work that takes the search for an old Zigong salt factory which disappeared nearly thirty years ago as its thread, recording through moving image the dialogues, misrecognitions, searches and connections between visiting creators and local residents. The work adopts an unconventional fieldwork method, placing the process of "searching" at its centre; the viewer follows the camera through a highly unusual local walk through Zigong.

Group 52342 | Can't Wake Up

The artist duo "52342" consists of Lesliecan and Fried Egg Tart. Their residency work Can't Wake Up departs from an old family compound house in a dream, using sleeping mats, salt water and needle and thread to evoke fragments of childhood, smell, domestic space and summer-holiday memory. When presented in the exhibition space, the work adopts a vertical hanging and curling approach, adding a sense of space and motion to compose a three-dimensional planar installation.

InsectSound | Seeing Mountains Not Mountains, Seeing Salt Not Salt

Occupying the largest corner of the entire left hall of Venue 2 is Seeing Mountains Not Mountains, Seeing Salt Not Salt by InsectSound. The work mainly uses large quantities of discarded glass material together with a self-welded derrick, as well as cat litter, granular soil and light panels from the artist's own daily life, with photographs scattered throughout, composing an imagined Zigong landscape. The artist also runs a café on a hill; the imagery of the mountain city and the memory of the salt capital appear simultaneously in the work — perhaps the shared perception of this city held by many Zigong-born youth.

Guan Jeff | 0.345 Grams of Memory

Guan Jeff is also an artist from the residency 1.0 phase. His distinctive work 0.345 Grams of Memory attracted considerable attention at the previous exhibition. By collecting his own sweat and evaporating it into crystals, the artist makes the salt within the body become the weight of memory. The coarse salt crystals change with the humidity of the air, and this change is registered on the electronic scale placed beneath them. The sources of salt as a material are wide-ranging; the artist turns his gaze from the external world back to himself, from "salt that can be taken" to "salt that can be precipitated" — a subtle and brilliant concept.

Lin Liying | Mustard Greens Leaves

Lin Liying, currently studying contemporary art in Germany, created Mustard Greens Leaves, an installation assembled from multiple materials. The scene of mustard greens hung out to dry — visible all across Zigong's streets and alleys — is appropriated by the artist into the exhibition site. The unseen scenes of everyday life in southern Sichuan are the image material most acutely felt, most often recorded and most often artistically transformed by the resident artists.

Ma Yiming | Connection

After viewing the left hall, which holds the largest number of works, we arrived at the final corridor space. Just inside the entrance, a straight bamboo pole reaching up to the ceiling caught the eye, its base inserted into the gap of a rust-covered tyre sunk into the ground. The work Connection, by Ma Yiming — a graduate of the Xi'an Academy of Fine Arts — uses found objects. "Connection" is an action that penetrates space; the juxtaposition of bamboo, a natural growth of the Ba-Shu region, with an industrial tyre is also a true portrait of this city. Here, the bamboo may also carry an additional layer of meaning: it is, as the curator noted in the preface, a metaphor for the "Castle in the Sky".

There are quite a few video works in the corridor. Xiaoyuer's Taste Laboratory (Very Salty · Very Idle) is a video interview that takes taste as its thread, exploring the relationship between local flavours in Zigong and individual memory; Li Jingming's Salt of Existence uses AI experimental moving image as its creative medium, threading through past, present and future to narrate, between reality and imagination, the bond between "salt" as a substance of existence and Zigong.

Seem | Wordless Dialogue

Seem is an intermedia creator from Shanghai. Her work Wordless Dialogue, created during the 1.0 phase of the residency, is presented through a combination of photography and installation. The body is her recent creative focus; during her time in Zigong she has continually sought connections between this city and herself. If the arrival of resident artists opens up different perspectives of observation and interpretation for this place, the destination likewise reciprocally shapes the creator's new self, new persona, new life experience.

Vivienne Peng | Little Salt Man: Art Salt-Spokesperson Vivi's Workshop, Plastic Traces

One of the works by Vivienne Peng (Peng Weiwen), initiator of the Diagonal Project and "The Salt of Life", is Little Salt Man: Art Salt-Spokesperson Vivi's Workshop — a new presentation of her "art commerce" series. Unlike previous iterations, this time she constructed a temporary livestream studio within the exhibition hall, where viewing, entering, consuming and interacting gradually unfold. Visitors could experience the scene of selling salt online and pay to take away the edible bagged salt scattered on the floor. Another performance-video work, Plastic Traces, was created many years ago; presented here alongside the new work, together they form a new "salt-man" theatre.

Lu Rui (Cuicui) | Flavour

At the deepest end of the corridor is the last work of the indoor part of the guided tour. Local educator and creator Lu Rui (Cuicui) continues the main line of her 1.0-phase practice, using thread, bells, salt, chairs and the body to construct a live performance. Starting from the complex and tense memories between the artist and Zigong, the work lets "flavour" become an experience jointly generated by body, city and emotion.

Live Art: Body, Salt and Public Space | 现场艺术：身体、盐与公共空间

After nearly an hour and a half of indoor guided tour, at 18:00 the audience stepped outdoors. The live art segment began across Venue 2 of the old street, the Water Theatre and the well to the west of the theatre.

First was Vivienne Peng's live piece Mining. The "well", an important spatial symbol in Zigong's salt-industry history, is re-translated into the site of a bodily action. The work extends the artist's long-term practice around salt, the body, production and family narrative; through the action of "mining", it reconnects the bodies of the public and the artist in participation and interaction, jointly completing the process of viewing — wishing — release.

The final finale of the opening ceremony was the live performance Realm by dancer Liu Shengyang. Presented as an environmental body theatre, the work takes bodily perception in open space as its core, allowing the dancing body to jointly compose the site together with water, bridge, riverbank, audience and the urban night. The body is no longer only the performer's body; it also becomes a medium for sensing the historical flow of a salt-industry city.

End of Opening Day: The Exhibition Continues to Unfold | 开幕日结束：展览仍在继续发生

At 20:00 in the evening, as the live art concluded, the opening day of "The Salt of Life" Contemporary Art Exhibition on Zigong Salt Culture came to a close for the time being.

From the opening speeches to the guided tour, from indoor works to outdoor live art, this day was not merely a starting ceremony, but a joint entry among art, place, body and public space. While viewing the works, the audience also entered the historical texture, lived site and urban memory of Ziliujing Old Street.

"The Salt of Life" salt-culture contemporary art exhibition enters the place through salt, touches memory through materials, and reorganises the relationships between people and people, people and city, people and history through art.

Salt is still here
The city is still growing
And the exhibition continues to unfold

Local Collaboration: A Co-Generated Public Site | 在地合作：一次共同生成的公共现场

The realisation of "The Salt of Life" could not have happened without the support of numerous local partner spaces and institutions. This exhibition received support from Ziliujing Old Street, the Zigong City Library, the China Salt Culture Research Center of Sichuan University of Science & Engineering, Liziran Art Space, DreamJIO, LANGKONG TIMETRAVEL, Dengxiashuo and other partner spaces and institutions.

The initiation and organisation of the exhibition also relied on the cluster of spaces the Diagonal Project has formed in Zigong, including SaltArt Space, Huojingtuo Creators' Dormitory and Carbon Studio. These spaces not only provide the foundation for residency, creation, display and public activity, but also enable the project to extend from a single exhibition into a long-term local cultural practice.
